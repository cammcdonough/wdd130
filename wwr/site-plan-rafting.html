Dry Oar Rafting Site Plan

Cameron McDonough

WDD 130 – Web Fundamentals

Overview

Site Name

Dry Oar Rafting

Purpose

The purpose of this site is to introduce people to whitewater rafting trips, explain our services, and help visitors feel comfortable booking an adventure with Dry Oar. The site highlights our rivers, safety, and how to contact us.

Audience

Our audience is families, young adults, and groups looking for a fun outdoor experience. Many visitors will be new to rafting, so content is simple, clear, and friendly. They will visit the site on both phones and computers, so the layout needs to be easy to read on all devices.

Branding

Website Logo

Style Guide

Color Palette

Primary: #396E94 (blue)
Secondary: #E7C24F (gold)
Accent 1: #A42212 (red)
Accent 2: #6f7364 (gray-green)

These colors are used for backgrounds, buttons, and accents to create an outdoor, adventurous feel.

Typography

Headings: Bebas Neue
Body Text: Open Sans

Headings are bold and simple. Body text is clean and easy to read.

Navigation

The navigation appears at the top of every page with links to: Home, Rivers, Site Plan, and Contact Us.

Site Map

Simple three-page structure:

  • Home – Overview of the company and main call to action.
  • Rivers – Information about different rivers and trip options.
  • Contact Us – Contact details and a form to send questions.

Wireframes

Home Page Wireframe

Hero image at the top with title and button, three feature sections (Rivers, Camping, Rapids), large image and message area, and footer.

Rivers Page Wireframe

Grid of river cards with images, titles, and text, followed by a “Book Now” call to action.

Contact Us Page Wireframe

Contact information on one side and a simple contact form with fields for name, email, and message.

Content Overview

Home Page

Short welcome message, explanation of who we are, highlight of what makes Dry Oar unique, and a strong button leading to booking or contact.

Rivers Page

Individual sections explaining each river (Snake, Colorado, Salmon) with a short description of difficulty level, scenery, and who it is best for.

Contact Us Page

Information about how to reach the company, when they can expect a response, and a simple message form.
